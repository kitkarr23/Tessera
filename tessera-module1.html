Culture signals
Two cultures matter here — the project team's and the organisation's. Both shape what is possible to design and deliver.
Practitioner reflection
"How has this organisation responded to change in the past — and does the project team know that history well enough to design for it?"
A starting hypothesis — not a fixed label. This will be tested and refined in the listening tour. Based on Bridges' Transition Model framing of organisational memory and endings.
Change-experienced
Has navigated multiple transformations. Has muscle memory — which can accelerate or create unhelpful shortcuts.
Change-fatigued
Too much change, too fast. Skepticism and low energy are likely early signals regardless of the quality of this initiative.
Change-naive
Limited transformation experience. May underestimate what transition actually costs people.
Change-scarred
A past change went badly. Trust in the process, leadership, or both is carrying damage.
This shapes how top-down messaging lands — and what genuine buy-in actually requires. Informed by Hofstede's power distance dimension and Meyer's leading scale.

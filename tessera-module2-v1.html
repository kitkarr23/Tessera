The human read
What the practitioner adds after the conversation. The things that don't show up in notes — but shape everything about how we design for this person.
Layer 2 — Practitioner debrief  ·  Within 60 minutes of the conversation
1
Question 1 — Emotional texture
"What did it feel like for them to come to work right now?"
Were they energised, stressed, guarded, burned out, curious? What was the emotional temperature of the conversation? Not what they said about it — what you felt from them.
2
Question 2 — Their change fingerprint
"How does this person handle change — and what does that look like specifically for this change?"
Not whether they resist or embrace change in general — what does their particular version of engagement or resistance look like in this context? What does it sound like, look like, feel like when it shows up?
3
Question 3 — What makes them tick
"What did you learn about this person that has nothing to do with work — and everything to do with who they are?"
What do they care about? What gives them energy? What are they proud of outside the org chart? This is where Brown's belonging principle lives — people engage with change when they feel seen as whole humans, not just role-holders.
Question 4 — The magic tick
"Did you find the frame that made this change land differently for them — the analogy, the connection, the reference point that made it suddenly real and relevant?"
This is the rarest and most valuable signal in the profile. It might be an analogy (like building a squad for a new season), a personal experience (a time they successfully navigated something similar), or a direct connection to something they care about. If you found it, capture it exactly. If not yet, flag it as something to find.
Magic tick found
Not what the project says their need is. What you actually heard — the thing the change plan must address for this person to move forward. This is your practitioner judgment, not a data point.
Complete profile
Both layers combined. This is the record that feeds every agent output for this person.

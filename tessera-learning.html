Learning & capability

Training is not a course. It is a designed experience that meets each person where they are — in their role, their language, their working environment, and their relationship to the change. Completion is not adoption. This unit designs for both.

Module 2  ·  Cohort design
Who trains together — and why it matters
Cohort groupings are not org chart groupings. The variables that matter: shared role in the new world, language and location, go-live sequence, technology access, union and regulatory constraints, and peer dynamics. A deskless warehouse worker in Brazil and a corporate analyst in New York may be going through the same system change — they should not be in the same training cohort.
Module 3  ·  Learning journeys & role cards
The path — and the person's place in it
A learning journey is a designed sequence — not a course list. Pre-work, core learning, practice, reinforcement, follow-up. The sequence matters. Role cards translate the journey into something a specific person can hold — in their hands or on their screen — and understand what this change means for them specifically.
Role cards — what this change means for each role

Role cards are designed deliverables — one per role group, printed or digital. They translate the change impact, the learning journey, and the support structure into language that feels personal rather than corporate. The creative output layer generates these as downloadable designed artifacts.

What gets built — and by whom
The training deliverables list — what the training workstream produces, in what format, owned by whom, and by when. Connected to the communications content library so training communications are not designed separately from everything else.
Module 5  ·  Delivery & scheduling
When training lands — and when it should not
Training scheduled into a saturation window does not land. A group cannot absorb a major communication and a two-day training in the same week. Scheduling is sequenced against the go-live date, the communications calendar, and the change saturation picture from Unit 2.

Module 6  ·  Adoption measurement
Did they learn it — or did they just attend?
Completion is not adoption. Five measurement tiers — from attendance to business outcome. Not all tiers apply to all groups. Union agreements, regulatory constraints, and workforce context determine what measurement is appropriate. The system captures the constraints and designs the measurement framework accordingly.
The five measurement tiers
Measurement design by group
Which tiers apply to which groups — and why. The practitioner confirms or adjusts. Flags are generated from the cohort technology profile, union status, and regulatory context captured in Module 2.
Connection to Unit 1 signal data
After major training events, update the Clarity and Capability signals for the relevant groups in the Unit 1 stakeholder map. Effective training should move the Clarity signal — if it does not, the training either did not land or reached the wrong people. The practitioner records the signal reading in Unit 1 with the training event as the trigger.

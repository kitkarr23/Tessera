Tessera™  ·  Unit 5  ·  Leadership engagement

What leaders need to do — not just say

Leadership is not the sponsor presentation. It is every individual interaction a leader has with every person on their team during this change. The people brief puts the whole person in the leader's hands — not as data, but as context for how to show up.

Module 1  ·  Sponsor map
Who is actually leading this change
Not the org chart. The actual humans who have made explicit or implicit commitments to lead this change — and whether their readiness, visibility, and actions match those commitments.
Module 2  ·  Coalition design
The leadership system — not just the sponsor
A single sponsor cannot carry a change. The coalition is the network of leaders at every level who are equipped, visible, and aligned — and who can reach the people that the executive sponsor cannot. This is where the middle management gap lives, and where most change programmes lose ground.

The people brief
Every leader gets a brief on the people they lead through this change. Not demographics — context. Who is this person, what are they carrying, what does this change mean for their specific situation, and how does this leader need to show up for them? The brief is generated from listening tour data and practitioner observations. The leader brings the relationship.
"The practitioner cannot be in every room. The people brief puts what the practitioner knows into the hands of the person who is."

Module 4  ·  Visibility plan
Where leaders show up — and what they do there
Leadership visibility is not the town hall appearance. It is the series of specific, intentional moments — in team meetings, on the floor, in 1:1s, in written messages — where a leader demonstrates through action what they said through words. The say-do gap is the most common trust-destroying pattern in change.
Module 5  ·  Sponsor action plan
The commitment — tracked
The sponsor action plan is not a one-time deliverable. It is a living document that tracks what leadership committed to, whether they did it, and what needs to happen next. It connects to the visibility plan and the signal data — if signals are not moving after a leadership action, the action may not have been visible or credible enough.

What we set out to understand
Project Meridian represents one of the most significant transformations Meridian Global has undertaken in over a decade — a simultaneous ERP implementation and organisational restructure affecting 340 people across four countries. The diagnostic phase was designed to answer five core questions before any change experience is designed: What does this population know? What do they feel? What are they worried about? What would make them ready? And crucially — what are they carrying alongside this change that the project team may not be seeing?
Twenty-eight interviews were conducted across six stakeholder groups over a four-week listening tour. This report reflects what was heard, what it means, and what needs to happen next.
What the data actually says
The headline number — 8% interview coverage across 340 people — understates both the depth and the gaps in what we know. Where we listened deeply, the data is rich and specific. Where we did not listen enough, the gaps are significant and must be named honestly before any design decisions are made.
The most important finding
The Finance Manager group is the highest-risk population in this engagement — not because they are resistant, but because they are carrying a wound from the 2020 ERP failure that has never been acknowledged. Their Commitment signal sits at 28%. Until trust is rebuilt, no communication, training, or engagement activity will land as intended. This is not a content problem. It is a relationship problem that must be addressed first.
The Supply Chain Frontline group — 84 people across the US and Brazil — represents the second critical risk. Clarity signal is at 40%, meaning the majority of this population does not yet have an accurate picture of what is changing for them. The Brazil sub-group has received no communication in Portuguese. The clock is running and the design window is narrowing.
Cultural complexity — what the signal data does not show
The signal data surfaces where groups are in their change journey. What it cannot show — and what this diagnostic must name explicitly — is the cultural interaction risk between the teams designing this change and the populations experiencing it. The project team operates predominantly with a US and UK cultural operating style: direct communication, data-led messaging, group-first sequencing, and efficiency-gain framing. Three of the six stakeholder groups — Finance Managers in France and Brazil, Supply Chain Frontline in Brazil, and Senior Leadership in France — operate with meaningfully different cultural orientations: relationship before task, indirect communication of concerns, individual-first information sequencing, and community-driven engagement. Without deliberate calibration, communications and training designed by this project team will feel foreign to the populations who need them most.
What we do not yet know
The Finance Frontline and Admin Support group — 196 people, the largest in the organisation — has two interviews completed. This is not enough data to design for. Any recommendation made for this group at this stage would be based on assumption, not evidence. Additional listening is required before design proceeds for this population.
The France population has two interviews total — both from management level. The France finance frontline team has no representation in the listening tour. The UK frontline has not been heard. These gaps must be closed before the diagnostic can be considered complete.
What needs to happen next
The recommendations that follow are sequenced by urgency and by what the data can currently support. Actions marked Do now address critical gaps that are already affecting the engagement. Actions marked Do soon build the foundation for effective design. Actions marked Plan for are important but can be properly sequenced once the immediate priorities are addressed.
Three things that must happen this week
1. Plain-language communication to Supply Chain Frontline in English and Portuguese — simultaneously, not sequentially. 2. Individual conversation with Finance Manager team leads acknowledging the 2020 ERP experience before any forward-looking messaging begins. 3. HR Business Partners briefed 48 hours ahead of the next general population communication — they are currently finding out at the same time as the people they are supposed to be supporting.

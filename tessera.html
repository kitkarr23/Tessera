What this is

A consulting delivery engine.

Most change management tools are built around the project. Tessera™ flips that. Every recommendation — every communication, every training experience, every leadership action — starts with a specific human being and their full context, and works outward from there.

The practitioner is always in the room. Tessera™ handles the analytical, generative, and synthesis work so they have the time and headspace to be fully human in the moments that require it.

Not mass personalisation. Designed dignity.

Tessera
Latin  ·  noun  ·  from Greek tessares
A single tile in a mosaic — complete in itself and essential to the whole. Every person in an organisation is a distinct piece. The job of change management is to understand each one and design an experience that honours that.

Six areas of practice

Everything a change practitioner needs.

From the first listening conversation to the final adoption measure — six areas, all built, all connected. Every recommendation in every unit draws from the same scenario data and the same cultural intelligence layer.

1
Discover
What is changing, for whom, and what the conditions are before a single conversation happens.
5 modules: Project context  ·  Listening tour with whole-person context  ·  Stakeholder signal map  ·  Experience design  ·  Diagnostic output with narrative and programme plan
In the Meridian demo: 28 interviews across 5 groups. Design hold active on Finance Frontline France — 196 people, insufficient listening data to proceed.
2
Analyse
The data picture — who is affected, how significantly, what the project documents tell you, and what they don't.
4 modules: Employee roster  ·  Project team directory  ·  Document intake & case for change  ·  Change impact analysis with heat map and pivot views
The CIA flags what the project has not yet answered and flows directly into training needs. Every flagged item carries through to Unit 4.
3
Communicate
Strategy through to tracking. Channel and message calibrated to cultural context, not one cascade for everyone.
7 modules: Comms strategy  ·  Message architecture with WIIFM by group  ·  Cascade model  ·  Plan & calendar  ·  Draft editor  ·  Tracking  ·  Content library with shared blocks
Different answers for a deskless worker in Brazil versus a senior leader in France. Shared blocks cascade updates across all deliverables automatically when something changes.
4
Learn
Culturally designed training. Completion is not adoption. Every learning decision tied to a specific person in a specific context.
6 modules: Training needs analysis  ·  Cohort design with language, device access and union flags  ·  Learning journeys by role  ·  Curriculum & scheduling  ·  Adoption measurement across 5 tiers
In the Meridian demo: Finance Frontline France (42 people) flagged for separate French-language cohort. Works Council consultation requirement surfaces before a single learning asset is built.
5
Engage
What leadership needs to do, not just say. Sponsor readiness is not assumed. The people brief puts the whole person in the room.
5 modules: Sponsor map with readiness and gap flags  ·  Coalition design  ·  People brief with whole-person context  ·  Visibility plan  ·  Action plan tracked against signal movement
In the Meridian demo: CFO Sarah Chen flagged — has not had the required 1:1 with Antoine D. in France before comms launch. The people brief surfaces what Marcus Webb needs to know about Rafael S. before walking into that conversation.
✦
Cultural intelligence
The foundation every other design decision is built on. Not a separate module — the lens through which every recommendation is made.
Six frameworks integrated: Meyer  ·  Hofstede  ·  Schwartz  ·  Trompenaars  ·  GLOBE  ·  Lewis  ·  Plus an inclusion and intersectionality layer across 7 categories
Inclusion categories from listening data: generational context, disability & access, gender dynamics, race & ethnicity, socioeconomic context, language & literacy, caregiving & life context. Design parameters, not demographic checkboxes.

What this is
A consulting delivery engine — not another CM tool.

Most change management tools are built around the project. Tessera flips that. The project is the context. The person is the center. Every recommendation — every communication, every training experience, every leadership message — starts with a specific human and works outward from there.

The practitioner is always in the room. Tessera handles the analytical, generative, and synthesis work so they have the time and headspace to be fully human in the moments that require it.

Tessera
Latin  ·  noun  ·  from Greek tessares
A single tile in a mosaic — complete in itself and essential to the whole. Every person in an organisation is a distinct piece. The job of change management is to understand each one and design an experience that honours that.

Unit 1 — Diagnostic foundation
Built
Five modules. One complete picture.
The diagnostic layer — project context, listening tour, stakeholder mapping, experience design, and diagnostic output. Everything that happens before a single communication is written or training is designed.
Module 1
Project context
Change landscape, strategic intent, organisational context, competing initiatives, change saturation, cultural signals, and practitioner assumptions before a single interview happens.

Module 2
Listening tour & person profiles
Design the interview guide, run structured interviews across three capture layers, build the person profile. Dynamic questions by role and project type. The practitioner's human read captured in the debrief layer.
Built→
Module 3
Stakeholder mapping
Groups the listening tour into meaningful segments. Signal heat map, coverage and gaps, cultural tension map, risk picture. Identifies where design hold applies before a change experience is built.
Built→
Module 4
Experience design
Tailored change experience recommendations per stakeholder group — communications approach, engagement strategy, learning design, adoption indicators. Every recommendation traces back to signals and interview evidence.
Built→
Module 5
Diagnostic output & dashboard
Five outputs from one source — visual dashboard, diagnostic narrative, high-level programme plan, presentation builder, and practitioner decision guide. Client branding applied throughout.

Unit 2 — Analysis & synthesis
Built
Four modules. Two people data layers.
The analysis layer — the impacted population, the delivery team, document intake, and the change impact analysis. Everything downstream in Units 3 through 7 reads from what is built here.
Module 1
Employee roster & people data
Import people data from Excel or CSV. Field mapping, data quality review, competing priority flags. Pivot views by department, location, and manager. Source of truth for audience mapping across all units.
Built→
Module 2
Project team directory
Sponsors, workstream leads, decision makers, and the CM team. Reviewer chains by deliverable type. Capacity flags. Engagement intelligence panel — emails, voice notes, uploaded documents, and channel threads analysed and surfaced for practitioner review. Full engagement version history.
Built→
Module 3
Document intake & case for change
Upload project documents — charter, business case, process maps, anything. AI extraction with confidence ratings. Open questions generated from gaps. The foundation the CIA builds from.
Built→
Module 4
Change impact analysis
Living record of what is changing, for whom, and how significantly. AI-generated first draft. Multi-select type, audience, and workstream. Heat map, pivot views, version history, open questions. Feeds communications, training, and leadership planning.

Unit 3 — Communications
Built
Six modules plus content library. The full communications system.
Strategy through to tracking. Not just email — channel strategy calibrated to cultural nuance and group signal data. Creative channel options surfaced when standard approaches will not reach the audience. Draft messages for leadership to react to before anything is finalised. Shared content blocks that cascade updates across all deliverables.

Communications strategy, message architecture & cascade model

Modules 4–6 · Plan, drafts & tracking
Communications plan, draft editor & tracking
Multi-layer calendar with project, programme, and organisation views. Time scales from week to multi-year. Swimlanes by audience group, workstream, phase, or owner. Full draft editor with approval states and version history. Signal movement before and after communications. Feedback themes and pulse check results.

What this is
A consulting delivery engine — not another CM tool.

Most change management tools are built around the project. Tessera flips that. The project is the context. The person is the center. Every recommendation the system generates — every communication, every training experience, every leadership message — starts with a specific human and works outward from there.

The practitioner is always in the room. Tessera handles the analytical, generative, and synthesis work so they have the time and headspace to be fully human in the moments that require it.

Tessera
Latin  ·  noun  ·  from Greek tessares
A single tile in a mosaic — complete in itself and essential to the whole. Remove one and the picture is incomplete. The name comes from Mosaic Bridge Advisory: every person in an organisation is a distinct piece. The job of change management is to understand each one and design an experience that honours that.

Unit 1 — Diagnostic foundation
Five modules. One complete picture.

Work through the modules in sequence for a full engagement, or jump directly to any module. Each one builds on the last — but all can be explored independently.

Module 1
Project context
The landscape before the listening begins. Nature of change, strategic intent, pressure and conditions, culture signals, change saturation, entry point, and practitioner assumptions. Generates early anchors that are stable before a single interview happens.
Built →
Module 2
Listening tour & person profiles
Three phases in one flow — design the interview guide, run the interview across three capture layers, build the person profile. Dynamic question generation by role, level, and project type. The practitioner's human read captured in the debrief layer.
Built →
Module 3
Stakeholder mapping
Groups the listening tour into meaningful segments. Five views — group profiles, signal heat map, coverage and gaps, ADKAR by group, all individuals. Risk picture, coverage picture, and readiness picture together and separately.
Built →
Module 4
Experience design
Tailored change experience recommendations per stakeholder group — communications approach, engagement strategy, learning design, adoption indicators. Every recommendation carries a source trail linking back to the signals and interview evidence that generated it.
Built →
Module 5
Diagnostic output & dashboard
Five outputs from one data source — visual dashboard with charts, diagnostic narrative, high-level programme plan, presentation builder, and practitioner decision guide. Client branding applied throughout. One source of truth.
Built →
